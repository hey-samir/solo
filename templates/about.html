Frequently Asked Questions

To log a route, tap the '+' button in the navigation bar. Fill in the details about your route including the color, grade, and whether it was a Send or a Try. Routes are tracked as either Sends (successful completions) or Tries (unsuccessful attempts).

A 'Send' means you successfully completed the route from start to finish without falling. A 'Try' is when you attempted the route but didn't complete it. Both are valuable to track as they show your progress over time. The total number of your Sends and Tries together represents all your Routes.

Points are calculated based on the difficulty of the route and whether you sent it or not. A Send earns you 10 points multiplied by your star rating, while Tries earn 5 points multiplied by your star rating. This system rewards both successful completions and the effort put into attempting challenging routes.

Your statistics show various aspects of your climbing progress:
  • Send Rate: The percentage of successful sends out of total routes attempted
  • Average Grade: The typical difficulty level you climb
  • Points: Your overall achievement score based on your routes
  • Sessions: Groups of routes climbed within the same timeframe
  • Star Rating: Your performance rating based on successful sends

When logging a Send, you'll record:
  • Grade: The difficulty rating of the route
  • Color: The hold color used for the route
  • Type: Whether it's a Send (success) or Try (attempt)
  • Stars: Your rating of the route quality
  • Date: When you climbed the route
  • Session: Which climbing session it belongs to
